Case 2 UUV Oceanic Surveillance

This example originates from [1]. Rather than considering single-objective optimization for the UUV scenario, we extended it to achieve multiple constraints under uncertainties and disturbances, while other parameters like sensor configurations are kept the same
The constraints to meet in this scenario are listed as below:

  • Scanning Distance ($C_l$): A segment of the surface over a distance of $L_t=100$ km is expected to be examined by the UUV within $\Delta=10$ hours, while the unrelaxable constraint of surveillance distance is $L=90$ km.
  • Energy Consumption ($C_E$): A total amount of energy $E_t=5.4$ MJ is expected to be consumed, while the maximum amount of energy is $E=6$ MJ.
  • Accuracy ($C_{\varphi}$): The accuracy of measurements is targeted at $A_t=90\%$, while the unrelaxable accuracy constraint is set as $A = 80\%$.


Experiment Results


1. Effectivess

At time instant $k = 100$, we change the target energy consumption ($E_t$) from 5.4 MJ to 5.0 MJ; at $k = 160$, we change the distance expected to be scanned ($L_t$) from 100 km to 105 km. The figure show that changes in constraints lead to rearrangement of the sensor usage arrangement, as the time portion for $S2$ increases. ACMP also reacts to changes in sensor parameters and sensor failures. At $k = 220$, the measurement accuracy of sensor $S3$ drastically decreases from $83\%$ to $43\%$, at $k = 290$, $S4$ stops working, and leads to a suboptimal solution that $S1$ is more exploited. Finally, the mission ends with average measurement accuracy of $90.1\%$, scanning distance of 106.7 km, and energy consumption of 4.98 MJ.


2. Robustness

The performance of ACMP and SCMP is compared while adding disturbances to sensors' parameters (i.e., sensor accuracy, scanning speed, and energy consumption) at random time instants. With ACMP, the UUV can scan a longer distance with higher accuracy and less energy consumption than SCMP.


3. Real-time Performance

The difference in average computation time between SCMP and ACMP is about 130ms in the quadrotor case and 7ms in the UUV case.


4. Choice of violation tolerance

Given a set of violation tolerances, the possibilities that relaxable constraints are reported as unsatisfiable are recorded per simulation under 9 times of random disturbances.

Experiment Setup

The Unmanned Aerial Vehicle hardware specifications are:

  • Vehicle Type
    DJI Matrice 100
  • Control Method
    DJI Mobile SDK
  • Sensor
    Camera: Zenmuse Z3 (resolution: $1920\times1080 \sim 4096\times 2160$, orientation angle $-90^\circ \sim 30^\circ$)
  • Laptop
    Intel(R) Core(TM) i7-7500U CPU@2.70GHz and NVIDIA GeForce 940MX
Normal Trajectory Following
  1. The UAV following the reference path with camera orientation down to the ground.
  2. The flight mission is expected to be completed within the time budget ($\Delta_t=24$ time steps) without any privacy risk.
  3. The UAV motion is discrete (up, down, left, right, forward, backward), and it moves at a constant speed of $0.5m/s$. The scale of the environment is $20\times20\times10m$, time step is $\tau=2s$, and prediction horizon is $N=2$. $\omega \in [33.3\%, 100\%]$, and orientation angles $-90^\circ$ (direct to ground) is represented with $\omega = 100\%$ and $30^\circ$ is $w = 33.3\%$.
  4. The approach is evaluated under the following tow settings:
    • Setting 1
      The deadline of the mission ($\Delta$) is set as 48 time steps, with the affected radius of the private region is $D_p=4.5m$.
    • Setting 2
      The deadline of the mission ($\Delta$) is set as 30 time steps, with the affected radius of the private region is $D_p=4m$.
Adaptive-Constrained Motion Planning with Setting 1

At the seventh time step, the UAV detects and locates the private house. With ACMP, the UAV analyzes whether the timeliness and privacy constraints can be satisfied simultaneously.

After constraint satisfaction checking and analysis, the UAV found that these two constraints cannot be satisfied at the same time and figured out an compromised resolution to go up, turning the orientation angle up to the sky without exposing the private house.

Finally, the UAV reaches the destination with 26 time steps, timeliness satisfaction of $DS^1(\mathcal{X}_\xi)=91.7\%$, and privacy preserving satisfaction of $DS^2(\mathcal{X}_{P})=96.6\%$.

Adaptive-Constrained Motion Planning with Setting 2

At the eighth time step, the UAV detects and locates the private house. With ACMP, the UAV analyzes whether the timeliness and privacy constraint can be satisfied.

After the constraint satisfaction checking and analyzing steps, the UAV finds the timeliness constraint can be guaranteed while the privacy constraint is unsatisfiable. It then changes the camera's orientation to avoid exposing the private house as much as possible while not changing the path.

Finally, the UAV reaches the destination with 24 time steps, timeliness satisfaction of $DS^1(\mathcal{X}_\xi)=100\%$, and privacy preserving satisfaction of $DS^2(\mathcal{X}_{P})=83.1\%$.

Notes
As the UAV is controlled by a customized Android application deployed on a remote controller, communication delay is taken into consideration such that the UAV will wait for the next command and take the action. In the future work, we plan to exploit the onboard SDK to save the communication time.
